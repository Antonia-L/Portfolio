Influential Guest Speakers and Field Trips

Guest Speaker: Jonathan Botkin

There were many guest speakers who had came to speak to the academy students, but the most memorable one for me is Mr. Jonathan Botkin. Mr. Botkin works for a solar energy company. He explained how solar cells work and he introduced some interesting engineering books. He also suggested students to get a job they like because it is easier to work with something you are interested in.
After Mr. Botkin's presentation, I learned more in depth about solar cells. It is useful for me because solar energy is becoming more popular and it is an inexhaustible energy. I also found out what books I should read that's about engineering. Mr. Botkins presentation also verified my thought that I should choose a job I like.

Guest Speaker: Jason Yosinski

Another guest speaker I had in my class was Mr. Jason Yosinski, he talked about machine learning with us. After his presentation, I learned how machines learn from their mistakes. This process improved over the last 10 years because of neronetwork, and it is an important process for artificial intelligents.
These are important knowledge for me because machine learning is getting more popular and it will be the direction the technology industry is going.

Field Trip: Patriot Jet Team Foundation

On 6 December, 2018, I went to the Patriot Jet Team Foundation on a field trip with other academy students. Patriot Jet Team Foundation hangar has a variety of different planes, and has plane simulators. During the trip, I learned about different features of a plane, I learned about emerging technologies in aerospace engineering, and I learned the ratio of the distance between the planets.
That field trip has taught me a lot about planes and how they function. Those are important information for me because my goal is to be an aerospace engineer. By learning about the principles of how a plane works, I can now know how to improve the design of a plane.

Field Trip: GoFly Event

On 29 February, 2020, I went to the GoFly Event. In that event, when I was looking at different designs for the flying machines, I noticed that they come in different shapes and sizes. They also used different amounts of propellers. One of my favourite ones is the one that shaped like a telephone booth. After looking at all of these designs, I realized that there are many different creative ways to approach a problem since I was expecting the final designs would all look like a miniplane more or less.
